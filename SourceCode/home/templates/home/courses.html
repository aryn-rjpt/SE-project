  1. 1.B.F.A. Painting

    Bachelor of Fine Arts in Painting is an Under-Graduate Painting course. In the Bachelor of Fine Arts in Painting program, the students are introduced to advanced painting techniques with an emphasis on theme development. They are taught to experiment with the form and content of the painting.

  2. 2.B.F.A. Sculpture

    Bachelor of Fine Arts in Sculpture is an Under-Graduate Fine Arts course. The Bachelor of Fine Arts in Sculpture is a fluid field of endless material possibility and can exist in multiple dimensions. It is no longer defined as pure form but as an inter-play of assembled forms involving a broad range of techniques and media.

  3. 3.B.F.A. Ceramics

    B.F.A in Ceramics and Pottery is a degree program which inculcates great expressive qualities in the domain of art and creativity. Initial years in pottery and ceramics, introduces the students to fundamentals of design to enhance their sensibility and sensitivity towards aesthetics and function of design.

  4. 4.M.F.A. Painting

    Master of Fine Arts in Painting is a Post-Graduate Painting course. Master of Fine Arts in Painting educates visual artists who create individual work and perform autonomous research with a view to gaining insight into new prospects for painting in a digital era based on a reflective and critical attitude. A degree in Painting is a credential that signifies a student's capacity to work independently in a discriminating, distinctive, and professional manner.

  5. 5.M.F.A. Sculpture

    Master of Fine Arts in Sculpture is a postgraduate Fine Arts course. The sculpture is a fluid field of endless material possibility and can exist in multiple dimensions. It is no longer defined as pure form but as an inter-play of assembled forms involving a broad range of techniques and media. The program provides a foundation or an extension for people who are, or would like to become, practicing artists in the visual arts or related fields.

  6. 6.M.F.A. Ceramics

    (M.F.A) degree in Ceramics is a concentrated three-year program devoted to studio, seminar, art history, and elective coursework. It emphasizes diversity, rigorous conceptual investigation, and contemporary critical discourse to develop individuals who challenge existing norms while producing compelling artwork. Through individual research, students develop skills, techniques and use of material as evidenced through a mature body of creative artwork.
